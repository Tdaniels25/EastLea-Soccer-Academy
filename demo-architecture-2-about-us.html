YOUTHS

Eastlea Soccer Academy

Giving Youths Roots To Grow And Wings To Fly For A Total Person Concept

Known as Coach George Jojo, I started my coaching career soon after hanging my boots in the late 90s. I was assigned to juniors team as a coach at Monomotapa United Football Club then known as Bindura United FC playing in the 1st division. That's when my passion for junior development was aroused.

It then grew from strength to strength until I started my own Eastlea Soccer Academy in 2005. At some point in 2006 I was appointed Head Coach at the Monomotapa United Football Club a feat which lasted for 8 months. I was reassigned to the juniors team as Coach working with Vainona High School where we enrolled our players of school going age. Tendai Samanja, Talent Gwaze, Tapiwa Ruzivo, Alexio Zhuwawo, Russel Madamombe are some of the players who later made it even internationally. I later churned out other stars the likes of Ocean Mushure, Praise Tonha, Marvelous Mukumba, Nelson Nelson, Ishmael Wadi, God knows Murwira. The fact that most of my juniors made it, motivated and inspired me to keep grooming youths.

Our motto of GIVING YOUTHS ROOTS TO GROW AND WINGS TO FLY FOR A TOTAL PERSON CONCEPT was later motivated by the fact that most of players were doing well at school because we encourage academic excellence besides thriving at soccer thus giving them more options later in life. Harare Children's Home, a local orphanage became our home also where we would go and train boys and girls in soccer. Occasionally donating food stuffs and clothing items as our outreach programs and our way of giving back to the community. Kids from this Home would come to train with us as a way of reintegrating them back into the society where they belong later in life. When we relocated to Ashdown Park across town such programs died a natural death because of distance and travel logistics. I have done outreach programs in the rural areas where I went to coach rural youths and talk about effects of drugs and teenage pregnancies.

Growing up in the rural setup I happened to witness alot of football talent going to waste because of no proper programs in place to identify, nurture, groom and dispose it to proper teams. I then moved into urban setup in the townships where alot of good talent went to waste because of drug abuse mainly because of lack of education on effects of drug abuse, lack of proper grooming when players would shun academics to concentrate on football yet they would be not ready for the grand stage

They would not know that football shelf life is short hence the need to plan properly the smooth transition from being a football star to an ordinary person who needs to survive and be responsible for their families. Later as a coach I witnessed brilliant soccer stars living vagrant lives because of financial mismanagement. These and many other experiences aroused my desire to help youths through academic and soccer excellence hence our motto WE GIVE YOUTHS ROOTS TO GROW AND WINGS TO FLY!!!

HAPPY PLAYERS

See What Young Talents Are Saying

RESPECT SPORTSMANSHIP AND GENERAL GOOD BEHAVIOR These attributes of a soccer player are inspired and motivated by experience with Zimbabwe Olympic Committee where Coach Jojo serves as a Volunteer Coach Ambassador. I train local teachers through workshops on Olympic and Futbolnet values which help us to shun and do away with violence in our society. Trained teachers will then go and train their kids whom they later bring to Olympic Centre for a festival and tournament to choose the best kids to display and demonstrate life values and life skills during competitions.

Kids are judged on values such as :Respect, Team work, Responsibility, Joy of effort/Excellence, Tolerance, Commitment and safe sport and the spirit of inclusivity. The winning team goes under Coach Jojo's supervision to compete representing Zimbabwe inthe SADC region. The Futbolnet programs are based on the FC Barcelona foundation model of mixed teams of boys and girls competing together.
